My Local Area: Skibbereen

Tragumna, Skibbereen, Co. Cork


I currently live in my native home place in West Cork. Like everyone from West Cork, I absolutely adore the place and could gladly sing its praises all day. I used to go to the water every single day either rowing, swimming or simply walking but since I quit work, ironically, I no longer have the time.


I live in a rather rural area, unfortunately however, living in the scenic countryside comes at a price and I am all too aware of this with the exhaustive commute I undertake each day.


Interestingly, it was only very recently that electrification came to rural West Cork, so things like computers are still a revelation to us down here. As of yet we are still waiting for broadband to arrive, but the word on the street is that it is imminently due, so the outlook for my computing interests is improving. As one may gather from all of this, living in rural West Cork is great in many respects, but it is not exactly ideal for a student in the field of computer science.


My Local City: Cork

Shandon Bells, Cork City


I currently study in my local city, Cork. I have never lived, studied or worked in this city previously. Until now, I have preferred to find to base myself in more far flung locations, or indeed be based back home in West Cork.


I have been trying to find rental accommodation in the city for many months but have been very unlucky thus far. Most people my age group are working and want housemates who are also working, whilst most landlords want a tenant who is full time employed.


All of the decent student accommodation appears to be taken - unless of course I am willing to part with a kidney to cover my rent in exchange for a room in an 8 bed new build apartment complex with young adults who do not conform to any sort of dish washing ideology. On average I spend about 4 hours a day on the road, mostly on buses, and so as a result of this I am slowly going crazy.
